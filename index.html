The Scriptures
of Rudon

"A world where power always has a biological price, where gods are imprisoned but their wounds are still bleeding, and where the only difference between a hero and a disaster is how well the former understands their own power before using it."

Biological Magic System Draconic Vocal Magic Faction Design 5 Continentes 20+ Plot Seeds Dietary Alchemy
Tone references: Sanderson (system) · G.R.R. Martin (moral ambiguity) · Abercrombie (consequence) · Rothfuss (magic as music) Language: English/Spanish Status: 90%

The Five Pillars — Core World Philosophy

I
Biological Price

All magic requires specific nutrients that the Magic Circuits convert into etheric substrate. Without nutrients → the Circuits cannibalize the mage's own tissues.

II
Knowledge First

First Immutable Law: A mage who does not understand their spell does not control it — they unleash it. Knowledge matters more than power.

III
Limits Define Character

What a being cannot do is narratively richer than what it can. Dragohism — the most powerful magic — has one absolute vulnerability: silence.

IV
Collective vs. Individual

Dragons build power for the nest; humans build it for themselves. Neither philosophy is superior — but they are incompatible enough to require active work and explicit agreements to collaborate.

V
No Invulnerability

The Fourth Immutable Law: there is always a weakness, a limit, a point of failure. The gods Dago and Nasgu had absolute power — and their greatest vulnerability was exactly that power.

Magic Systems Designed

  • Kinergia — Internal vital energy; redesigns the body from within. Inconvertible to external energy.
  • Ethernergia — External cosmic energy; alters reality outward. Five-Circle mastery framework.
  • Dragohism — Vocal magic integrated into the draconic vocal apparatus. Five tonal registers with radically different effects.
  • Hirgh (Vile Magic) — Uses corrupt energy of the Vacuous; produces progressive cognitive degradation.

Factions & Political Design

  • Metallic Dragons — Twelve centuries of historical loyalty fracturing under internal disagreement over human integration.
  • Matte Dragons — Right in the diagnosis, wrong in the response. Generational tension between elders and youth.
  • The Arron Empire — Efficiency as religion. Excellent at known questions; blind to unknown threats.
  • Kingdom of Alexandria — A 400-generation secret so old the guardians themselves no longer know what they know and what they don't.
  • Monks of Kinergia — The only faction without expansive ambition, in constant struggle to protect the world.
  • The Tatorath — Invisible corrupted infiltrators. Indistinguishable except they never eat.

Narrative Themes & Macro-Design

  • Knowledge as Responsibility — Whoever knows more has greater obligation over what they do with that knowledge.
  • The Price of Being What You Are — Power leaves permanent physical marks. No one becomes something without it leaving traces.
  • The Impossible Collaboration — The Seal Song was the only time all factions fully cooperated. Can it happen again?
  • Power Without Purpose — The nestless queen holds the most advanced power in the world and no reason to use it.
  • 20 Plot Seeds — All documented with systemic consequence chains

Hard Sci-Fi · Space Opera · Narrative Bible

Universe
Planet Blue

A galactic civilization where democracy erodes into corporate feudalism wherever quantum communications don't reach — and secrets are the most valuable currency in the universe.

30+ Especies 5 Major Factions 6 Religion Systems Real Physics Applied Political Economy 21 Sections
Tone: Hard Sci-Fi + Space Opera Drama · NASAPUNK Aesthetic (Federation) · Fetish Aesthetic (Wilstoide) · Neo-Bushido (Saito) Language: English/Spanish Scale: Galactic — 40+ cluster systems with their respective planets

Technology Systems Designed

  • Warp Engine (Alcubierre-Casimir) — Based on real 1994 physics. One activation equals Earth's annual electricity output. Restricted to military and elite use.
  • Quantum Entanglement Network — A pre-existing dimension within the galactic equivalent of the Internet. Accessing it with a full-control interface leaves the physical body completely defenseless.
  • Mara Modular Ships — Circular modular architecture inspired by a real Venezuelan architect from 1972. The design philosophy shapes the entire aesthetic identity of the Federation.
  • UMPA Exoskeletons — Repurposed industrial units. The underworld's warship. Four tactical variants, each with its own doctrine.
  • Stellar Battery — Only one exists. Irreplicable. The Federation's most classified secret, powering humanity's mothership.

World-System Design Decisions

  • AI Prohibition — The AI War drew the legal boundary between Artificial Intelligence (banned) and Virtual Intelligence (controlled). The self-replicating machine factory was never found.
  • Espers — Inter-species hybrid individuals with anomalous cognitive abilities. Detained on a secret planet whose existence the Federation officially denies.
  • Wormholes as Narrative Engine — Natural, uncontrollable, unpredictable. When one closes, it can isolate an entire cluster forever. The tragedy of the Human and Maint Clusters results from this.
  • Feudal Periphery — Clusters without quantum nodes operate in a different political reality: no central authority, no standard currency, no military protection.
  • Q / "The Architect of Chaos" — A single unidentified individual who temporarily collapsed the Federation using only information, money, and time. Identity unknown. Motivations unknown. Still at large.

Six Religion Systems Designed

  • Eudaimonism — Synthesizes six philosophical currents (Humanism, Altruism, Stoicism, Determinism, Hedonism, Idealism) into a lived practice. Libraries and observatories are its temples.
  • Tenguer — Martial-religious order: combat as the path to spiritual clarity. The Butterfly Swords are their greatest secret — a dual weapon capable of cutting through any material, including Warp ring generators.
  • Path of the Bond — The ethics of a bond you cannot revoke. The Federation requires 90 days of waiting. The Bonded reply: "Death is also irreversible and nobody asks consent for birth."
  • Eyes of the Deep — The Lateo species' skin secretes a substance that produces mystical states in other species. They never asked for this. The ethical debate of what your biology does to other minds.
  • Grand Hermeneutics — The universe as an infinite text. The Threshold Cults read meanings that destroy sanity in ordinary minds.
  • The Scaly Design — The Wilstoide theocracy: racial supremacy as divine mandate. Legally classified as hate ideology in federal space.

Narrative Arcs & Open Tensions

  • The Maint Tragedy — The Ben Ari family seat, the universe's greatest transporter, is in a cluster isolated forever. The heir's daughter is imprisoned in the Federation's secret genetics facility. What does she sacrifice to save her?
  • The Saito Succession — Five heirs, five possible futures for the human empire: federation, vassalage, religious theocracy, reform, or chaos. The Wilstoide have already bet.
  • Espers and Academia — The Federation's greatest secret is also its greatest ethical shame. The day it becomes public, no institutional legitimacy survives.
  • The Eoalo Mystery — Why do they observe and never intervene? What does the Gamma frequency contain? Are the wormholes their creation — or their responsibility?
  • Q's Return — "Q didn't leave. Q is waiting. The pizza is reheating." An open arc designed to destabilize the universe-level status quo at any narrative moment the team decides.

Core System: The Vampiric Impulse

The central mechanical tension: vampires are cursed with an insatiable need to drink human blood — but doing so degrades them, costing power and status. A Yuán zǔ (primordial) who yields becomes a lesser Lord. Recovery requires centuries of complete abstinence.

This creates a built-in internal conflict for every vampire regardless of alignment: their greatest temptation is also the thing that makes them weaker and easier to kill.

Operational Doctrine

  • Phase Zero — No hunt begins without: target intelligence, terrain recon, nest identification, incursion plan with contingencies.
  • Anvil and Hammer — Two-team pincer: one seals the main exit, the other drives the target from secondary routes into the Anvil's trap.
  • The Hour Before Dawn — Optimal assault window when targets are fatigued and forced toward their lair.
  • Pursuit Rule — Pursuit is a failure of planning. Only under specific conditions (silver-wounded, open terrain, dawn imminent).
  • Velum Protocol — If a civilian witnesses combat, the primary objective shifts from eliminating the vampire to securing the witness.

Legal & Ethics Framework

  • Authority derives from the Velum Salomonis — a global concealment spell — not from any state.
  • Zero civilian casualties is a categorical imperative, not a preference.
  • Killing a civilian witness is punishable by expulsion and death.
  • Neutral clans are never attacked without provocation — Active Surveillance only.
  • A hunter who causes a Breach of the Veil must undertake an Expiation Rite: a solo mission to correct their error or die trying.
  • Three documented case studies (Night Eagle, The Prague Error, The Sevastopol Trap) used in training doctrine.

04   What This Work Demonstrates

Consistency as a Design Principle

Every system in my worlds operates the same way for everyone. Rudon's Immutable Laws apply to gods and students alike. Warp physics apply whether you are the Federation or a mercenary. Internal rules are non-negotiable — and that is what creates trust in a fictional world.

Moral Complexity Without Both-Sidesism

No faction in any of my projects is simply evil. Rudon's Matte Dragons are right in the diagnosis and wrong in the response. The Federation imprisons Espers out of fear, not malice. The Vonderburg clan hunts the monster that was once their own blood. Complexity is structured, not vague.

Systemic Plot Generation

My worlds don't contain stories — they generate them. Rudon's 20+ plot seeds each pull on a different systemic thread. The open arcs in Blue Planet (Q, the Maint Cluster, Academia, the AI factory) are designed to destabilize the world at any narrative moment the team decides.

Documents Written for Other Creators

A narrative bible is useless if only the author can navigate it. All three projects include explicit adaptation guides for creators, tone notes, sensory design references for illustrators and game masters, and visual guides for designers — written for cross-disciplinary teams.
